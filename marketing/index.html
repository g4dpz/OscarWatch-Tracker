For VHF/UHF satellite operators

Track the pass.
Work the bird.
Let the shack keep up.

OscarWatch is a free desktop app for radio amateurs working AMSAT spacecraft: FM cubesats, linear transponders, and the usual Oscar fleet. One map-centred window for pass times, live azimuth and elevation, Doppler-corrected frequencies, and optional rotator and radio automation.

Download for Windows, macOS & Linux View on GitHub
  • Free & open source (AGPL)
  • No cloud account required

Built for operators who speak satellite

You know what AOS, TCA, and Doppler mean. OscarWatch does the orbit maths and station chores so you can concentrate on the contact, whether in the shack or portable.

FM & digitals

SO-50, ISS, AO-91 and friends: pass list, sky plot, CTCSS access and arm tones, live az/el pointing.

Linear transponders

Mode B/J/V modes from the built-in database, live uplink and downlink with Doppler, TX/RX offsets.

DX skeds & portable

Pass planner with portable profiles, mutual-pass finder for two-station skeds, and iCalendar export for your calendar.

Everything you need around one pass

Tracking, planning, and station assist, without replacing your logger or decoding telemetry.

World map & sky plot

Subpoint, ground track, footprint, and a polar sky view from your QTH. Click to focus a satellite.

Pass predictions

Upcoming passes with max elevation, duration, and countdown to AOS. Filter by min elevation and length.

Doppler frequencies

Draggable overlay with transponder modes, live radio and satellite frequencies, and split offsets.

Radio CAT

Built-in rig drivers with Doppler tracking, satellite/split layout, Main/Sub VFOs, and uplink CTCSS. No external CAT software.

Rotator tracking

Built-in GS-232 and EasyComm drivers for pass tracking, park, 450° smart azimuth, and manual control between passes.

Planning tools

Multi-station pass planner, mutual-pass finder for two grids, and transponder database merge from tle.oscarwatch.org.

Voice alerts

Optional spoken “satellite rising” announcements at a configurable elevation, for example “Alpha Oscar Zero Seven is rising”.

Pass recording

Automatic WAV capture from line-in or USB audio while the focused bird is above your thresholds.

Cloudlog sync

Optional Radio API posts of SAT uplink and downlink while you are tracking, if you use Cloudlog.

From cold start to QSO

A typical pass workflow; most operators are on the air in minutes.

Set your QTH

Enter latitude, longitude, and Maidenhead grid. Refresh TLEs from tle.oscarwatch.org.

Pick your birds

Enable the satellites you plan to work. Choose transponder mode and CTCSS where needed.

Point & tune

Follow az/el on the sky plot or let the rotator track. Set frequencies manually or via CAT.

Resume & work

Leave Standby before the pass. Focus the satellite, work the contact, park when done.

Built-in drivers for rigs and rotators you already own

OscarWatch includes its own radio and rotator drivers for direct serial CAT and rotator control from one app, with no third-party control software in the chain.

Supported radios (built-in drivers)

  • ICOM IC-910 / IC-9100 / IC-9700 (CI-V)
  • Yaesu FT-847 (Yaesu CAT)
  • Kenwood TS-2000 (beta, SATL mode)
  • Dummy rig for testing without hardware

Supported rotators (built-in drivers)

  • Yaesu GS-232 and compatible clones
  • EasyComm II (SPID, M2, and similar)
  • 360° and 450° azimuth ranges
  • Calibration offsets and smart north crossing

Windows, macOS, and Linux

Pre-built release packages for x64 and ARM64. Extract and run: no account, no subscription.
