Upcoming Passes (sidebar)

The quickest view while operating. Shows passes for enabled satellites above your minimum elevation (Settings → Tracking). Click a pass to select that satellite.

TCA is time of closest approach — usually the best time in the pass. Badges show when a pass is soon (orange countdown), in progress (green Passing), or being recorded (red REC). See Map & sidebar for details.

Pass planner

Passes → Pass planner opens a larger window for planning:

  • Choose a station profile (home, portable, etc.) with its own coordinates.
  • Set minimum elevation and minimum pass duration to filter weak or short passes.
  • See a table of passes over a longer prediction window.
  • Export calendar (.ics) — add passes to Outlook, Google Calendar, or your contest plan.

Mutual pass finder

Passes → Mutual pass finder finds times when a satellite is visible from two stations at once — useful for QSOs between two grids or for rovers. Enter the remote operator’s Maidenhead grid (and an optional callsign or name), set your filters, then press Find mutual passes. The table lists each satellite with the mutual window, overlap duration, and pass times at both stations.

For live azimuth and elevation at a remote grid while you operate, use the DX station monitor on the map (Map & sidebar).

Opening the visualizer

  • Double-click a pass row to open the mutual pass visualizer.
  • Or right-click a row and choose Open visualizer.

The visualizer shows side-by-side polar plots (west station on the left, east on the right) with the pass path drawn in sunlit and eclipse segments. Green diamond markers show mutual window start; orange circle markers show mutual window end. Hover along a pass track to see a tooltip with time, azimuth, and elevation, and a blue dot on the path showing where you are on the plot. Use the checkboxes to overlay each station’s full pass instead of only the mutual window. Screenshot saves the whole visualizer window as a PNG.

Copying pass details

Right-click a pass row and choose Copy to put a formatted text summary on the clipboard — satellite, both stations, mutual window, pass times, max elevation, and AOS/LOS azimuths. Paste it into an email or message to coordinate a sked. Times follow the local/UTC setting in the mutual pass window.

Sunlight prediction

Passes → Sunlight prediction shows when a satellite is in sunlight or eclipse over a period. Helpful for modes that need illumination or for planning FM shifts.
