Main settings

  • COM port and rotator type.
  • SPID native mode: set the controller to SP protocol and Auto (A) on the front panel before connecting.
  • Azimuth range — 360° or 450° (G-5500 style).
  • Smart azimuth (450°) — shortest path across north when useful (only shown for 450°).
  • Elevation range — 0–90° standard, or 0–180° for over-the-top mounts that can track past zenith by flipping azimuth.
  • Track-start elevation — tracking begins when the satellite rises above this angle (default −3°).
  • Park azimuth / elevation — home position.
  • Park rotator after pass — on by default; moves to park when the satellite drops below track-start. Uncheck to leave the beam where it finished.
  • Calibration offsets — optional az/el corrections (degrees) for pass tracking and manual moves. Park uses configured park az/el exactly.

Keyhole avoidance (0–180° elevation only)

On 0–180° rotators, a high pass near zenith can outrun the rotator — the “keyhole” where azimuth swings rapidly while elevation passes through 90°. OscarWatch can analyse such passes and, when beneficial, pre-position the beam flipped (azimuth +180°, elevation 0°) before AOS, then track over the top instead of fighting the keyhole.

These options appear only when elevation range is 0–180°. They are hidden for 0–90° rotators.

  • Enable keyhole avoidance — off by default. Sidebar may show Keyhole Avoidance during a flipped pass.
  • Slew rate (°/s) — your rotator’s maximum speed; used to estimate signal-loss windows.
  • Keyhole threshold (°) — minimum pass max-elevation (60–89°) before a pass is treated as keyhole (default 80°).

If planning fails or the feature is off, tracking uses normal az/el commands.

Standby and manual pointing

Standby parks the rotator and pauses CAT — use when browsing the map. Resume restores pass tracking.

While in Standby, the menu bar shows Rotator (if rotator control is enabled). Point the beam manually without resuming tracking — handy for a quick terrestrial contact or between passes.

  • Enter Azimuth and Elevation within your configured limits.
  • Rotate — slew to position; Stop — halt in progress; Park — go to park.
  • Close — dismiss the dialogue; beam stays put until Park or Resume.
