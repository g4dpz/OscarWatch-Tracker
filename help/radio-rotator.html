OscarWatch can steer your beam and keep your radio on frequency during a pass. You do not need to understand every setting on day one — pick your radio below and read that page first.

Before you plug in

  • Use a USB serial lead (or proper interface) to the radio and rotator.
  • Radio and rotator need different COM ports — and a third port if you use GPS.
  • Close other programs that might hold the port (HRD, WSJT-X rig control, Hamlib, etc.).
  • For normal cross-band satellites, your radio should be in satellite mode when tracking starts (OscarWatch usually sets this), except the Kenwood TS-2000 — you must select SAT yourself first.
  • For receive-only modes (ISS SSTV, beacons — uplink 0 in the database), you do not need satellite mode; OscarWatch tunes the downlink only.

One radio or two?

Single radio — one physical rig handles both receive and transmit (ICOM satellite rigs, FT-847, TS-2000). OscarWatch uses satellite mode or split as your rig requires.

Dual radio — downlink on one radio, uplink on another (common: FT-818 for RX + IC-706 for TX). Enable Settings → Radio → Dual radio and set a COM port for each leg. See Yaesu dual or ICOM dual for wiring and baud rates.

What happens during a pass

When you track a satellite and CAT is enabled, OscarWatch:

  • Sets the right modes and satellite layout at pass start (rig-dependent).
  • Updates receive and transmit frequencies as Doppler changes.
  • Applies your RX and TX offsets from the frequency panel.
  • Sends CTCSS when your radio and satellite mode support it.
  • Steers the rotator (if configured) while the satellite is above your track-start elevation.

Standby in the sidebar parks the rotator and pauses CAT — use it while browsing the map. Press Resume when you are ready to operate.

Receive-only modes (SSTV, beacons)

When uplink is 0 in the database (e.g. ISS SSTV), the frequency panel shows uplink as a dash. OscarWatch only corrects the downlink.

  • ICOM IC-910 / IC-9700 / IC-9100 / IC-821H — turns satellite mode off, puts the downlink band on Main, applies Doppler there only.
  • Other rigs — receive frequency only; layout depends on the driver.

After a voice satellite pass, switching to ISS SSTV is enough — OscarWatch reconfigures at the next track start.

Linear CW note

On linear SSB transponders, the Voice / CW buttons in the frequency panel choose how CAT sets modes. With Linear CW: keep receive in USB/LSB checked in Settings (usual), transmit goes to CW and receive stays in sideband. See Frequencies and Shortcuts (Ctrl+W).
