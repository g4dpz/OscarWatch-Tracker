Who is this for?

OscarWatch is for licensed amateurs who work satellites on VHF and UHF: FM repeaters, SSB transponders, and similar modes. You do not need to be a software expert. If you know what a pass, azimuth, elevation, and Doppler shift are, you are in the right place.

What OscarWatch does

  • Shows where satellites are on a world map and a sky plot at your station
  • Lists upcoming passes over your location
  • Works out uplink and downlink frequencies (including Doppler) from a transponder database
  • Can steer your rotator and update your radio on CAT during a pass (optional)
  • Can announce when a satellite is rising (optional voice)
  • Can record pass audio to WAV when a satellite is above a set elevation (optional)

Five-minute setup

  1. Open Settings and enter your station position (latitude, longitude, and height if you know it).
  2. Go to Satellites → Select satellites and tick the spacecraft you want to work.
  3. Use Satellites → Refresh TLEs so orbital data is up to date.
  4. Click a satellite on the map or in the pass list to focus on it.
  5. During a pass, use the frequency panel on the map for modes, offsets, and (if configured) CAT tracking.

See Quick start for a typical field-day workflow.

Tip: Use Standby in the sidebar when you are only browsing the map. It parks the rotator and pauses CAT so you do not send accidental commands to your hardware. While in Standby, menu Rotator lets you point the beam manually for a quick contact between passes.

Where files are stored

On Windows, OscarWatch keeps your data under:

%AppData%\OscarWatch\

That folder holds settings, TLE cache, your edited transponder database, pass recordings, and log files. Use Help → Open logs folder in the app if support asks for a log.
