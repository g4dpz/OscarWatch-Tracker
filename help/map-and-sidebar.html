World map

The map shows the satellite sub-satellite point (where it is over the Earth), its ground track, and a footprint (rough visibility circle). Your station is marked on the map.

  • Click a satellite label or icon to select it.
  • Tab to the map, then use arrow keys to change the selected satellite.
  • Coloured footprints help you see which satellites are near your horizon.

Sidebar — Live

Shows data for the selected satellite, updated every second (UTC clock at the bottom).

ItemMeaning
Standby / ResumeBrowsing mode: parks rotator, pauses CAT
Az / ElDirection to point your antenna (degrees)
Range / AltDistance to the satellite and its altitude
Sunlight / EclipseWhether the spacecraft is sunlit (when data is available)
RotatorLive az/el from your rotator controller, if connected
ParkSends park command to the rotator
RadioLast CAT-reported RX/TX, or overlay frequencies

Sky plot

A polar view as seen from your station: centre is overhead, the outer ring is the horizon. Click a satellite to select it, same as on the map.

Upcoming passes

A list of predicted passes for your enabled satellites. Times use your local display; the footer shows UTC. Click a row to select that satellite. Passes above your minimum elevation (Settings → Tracking) are included.

Status bar

Shows how old the TLE data is and how many satellites are enabled. If TLEs are very stale, refresh them before relying on pass times.
