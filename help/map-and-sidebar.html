World map

The map shows the satellite sub-satellite point (where it is over the Earth), its ground track, and a footprint (rough visibility circle). Your station is marked on the map.

  • Click a satellite label or icon to select it.
  • Tab to the map, then use arrow keys to change the selected satellite (keyboard shortcuts).
  • Coloured footprints help you see which satellites are near your horizon.
  • Optional day/night greyline (Settings → Appearance) shades the night side of the map; footprints and tracks draw on top and follow map time when you scrub.
  • A small arrow inside each footprint shows the direction the satellite is moving along its ground track.
  • Your station is marked on the map. A saved DX station (remote grid) appears as an orange diamond.
  • Press S to show only the selected satellite on the map and sky plot; press S again to restore all (shortcuts).

DX station monitor

Use this when you are working a contact at another location — for example a rover in a different grid — and want to see where they are and whether the focused satellite is up in their sky.

  1. Click the small orange diamond button at the top-right of the map.
  2. Enter a Maidenhead grid square (4 or 6 characters, e.g. JO22 or JO22lk).
  3. The remote location is marked on the map. A compact panel shows Az (their sky) and El (their sky) for whichever satellite you have selected, updated every second.

Drag the panel by its title bar (⠿) to move it. Use ▼ / ▶ to collapse or expand, same as the frequencies panel. OscarWatch remembers the grid, panel position, and collapsed state between sessions. Press Clear to remove the remote station and close the panel.

If a grid is already saved, the map marker stays visible when you start OscarWatch; click the diamond button again to open the compact monitor. For pass times visible from two stations at once, use Passes → Mutual pass finder (pass planning).

Sky plot

A polar view as seen from your station: centre is overhead, the outer ring is the horizon. Click a satellite to select it, same as on the map. Expand or collapse the section with the Sky plot header — OscarWatch remembers which you prefer.

Upcoming Passes

A collapsible list of predicted passes for your enabled satellites. Expand or collapse the section with the Upcoming Passes header — OscarWatch remembers which you prefer. Times use your local display; the footer shows UTC. Click a row to select that satellite. Passes above your minimum elevation (Settings → Tracking) are included. When expanded, the list fills the space between Live/Sky plot and any roves panel; it scrolls when there are many passes.

Badges on the right of a pass row:

BadgeMeaning
Orange countdownPass starts within about 15 minutes (time until AOS)
Green PassingPass is in progress (between AOS and LOS)
Red RECAudio is being recorded for this satellite (Settings → Recording)

Rows may also be highlighted in amber (soon) or green (in progress) behind the text.

Upcoming Roves (hams.at)

When enabled in Settings → Integrations, a collapsible Upcoming Roves panel appears at the bottom of the sidebar. It lists satellite rove alerts from hams.at that are workable for your hams.at location (is_workable in the API) — callsign, grids, time window, satellite, and any comment. Set your observer location on hams.at if the panel is empty. Click a row to open that alert on hams.at in your browser. If Cloudlog is enabled in Settings → Integrations, use Test connection to load your logbooks and pick one — rove grid badges then show red grids you still need and muted badges for grids already in that logbook (satellite contacts only).

Rove grid badges

When Cloudlog grid check is enabled and a logbook is selected:

BadgeMeaning
Red pillGrid(s) you still need — not found in the chosen logbook on SAT
Muted outline pillGrid(s) already worked on satellite in that logbook
Green pillAll grids shown together when Cloudlog lookup is off (no logbook selected or checkbox off)

A rove with multiple grids may show two badges side by side — for example red NM48 and muted NM49 when you need one grid but have already worked the other. Several grids in the same category are combined in one badge (e.g. red NM48, NM49).

Enter your hams.at API key and refresh interval on the Integrations tab (stored locally like other credentials). Expand or collapse the section with its header; OscarWatch remembers your preference. When expanded, drag the grip at the top of the roves list to make it taller or shorter — your chosen height is saved automatically.

Status bar

Shows how old the TLE data is and how many satellites are enabled. If TLEs are very stale, refresh them before relying on pass times.

When GPS is enabled in Settings → Integrations → GPS, coloured labels appear to the left of the map-time buttons:

  • GPS — green = valid fix; red = GPS on but no fix yet (or lost).
  • GPS Time — shown only when Use GPS UTC for satellite tracking is on. Green = tracking is using GPS UTC; red = waiting for a fix with valid time.

Hover a label for a short tooltip. GPS indicators are hidden when GPS is disabled in Settings.

On the right, next to the UTC clock, map time buttons let you preview footprints and satellite positions without changing real hardware control: −15m, −5m, Now, +5m, and +15m step the map and sky plot backward or forward (up to about two hours from live time). Press Now to return to the current time.

  • While previewing, the UTC clock shows the map time and how far it is from now.
  • The sidebar azimuth, elevation, range, and sunlight status follow the preview time.
  • Rotator, radio CAT, pass recording, and voice announcements stay on live time so you do not drive the station from a preview.
